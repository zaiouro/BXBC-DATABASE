Blockbears Presents
THE SHOW
A tragedy in five acts
Every story needs a stage.
Ours was a pizzeria inHurricane, Utah. Brand new. Bright lights. Happy music. The kind of place where children laughed and parents relaxed and nobody ever asked questions.
The animatronics stood on stage and played their instruments and smiled. They smiled because that's what they were built to do.
Act I
THE BUILDER
William Afton built the springlock suits with his own hands. Fredbear. Spring Bonnie. Bonabi. He knew exactly how they worked. He knew exactly what happened when they failed.
"Hey there kid! Why don't you follow me, Springbonnie, to get some cake!"
He always smiled. Always.
Act II
THE FIRST ACTOR
Victor Callahan was eighteen. He performed in the Fredbear suit at his sister's birthday party. He told terrible jokes. He made children laugh.
"No need to be shy! I don't bite. Well... at least not anymore."
He was the kind of person who makes you feel brave. Even when he was scared. Especially when he was scared.
1983
THE ACCIDENT
Holly was six. She pulled a lever she wasn't supposed to touch.
The springlocks collapsed inside the Fredbear suit. Her brother bled out inside the mascot head while children sang happy birthday on the other side of the curtain.
He never blamed her. Not once. Not even now.
She never forgave herself.
THE SMILE
William Afton didn't mourn. He saw an opportunity.
He murdered Henry's daughter. Henry vanished. William took control.
Something broke inside him that day. Or maybe something was always broken, and this just let it out.
He put on the suit. He put on the smile.
Act III
THE HUNTING
He lured them with cake. With promises. With a smile they trusted.
Oscar vanished first. Then Joshua went looking. Then Trixie.
He stuffed them into the machines. One by one. Year after year. Their souls trapped inside the metal. They couldn't leave. They couldn't forget.
"WHY WAS I MADE ONLY TO FALL APART LIKE THIS?"
THE TRAPPED
At night, the machines move. They walk the halls. They search for something they can't name.
Holly screams Victor's name. Trixie calls for Oscar. Joshua stands guard. Victor hides in the basement, smiling, always smiling.
They are still there. They have always been there.
If you listen closely at 3 AM... you can hear them.
1993
THE DISAPPEARANCE
Afton stopped killing. Before leaving, he murdered an employee who had uncovered secrets that could not be let out.
He partnered with Chuck E. Cheese and vanished for years to let the heat die down.
The children's souls are still inside those machines.
He knows.
He doesn't care.
Act IV
THE RETURN
He came back. Still performing. Still smiling. Still wearing the suit.
New staff. New animatronics. Same souls inside. Nobody told them what happened before. Nobody tells them what moves at night.
"If we're all stuck here together... then we should at least try to smile."
Oscar still tries to make people smile. Even now. Even like this.
Act V
THE PRESENT
The janitor knows something is wrong. The visitor keeps coming back. The researcher studies what shouldn't be studied.
The animatronics are still trapped. Holly still screams. Trixie still calls. Victor still hides. Joshua still guards.
The show is almost over.
But the curtain hasn't fallen yet.
THE SHOW
The curtain never closes.
The show never ends.
The dead are still watching.
